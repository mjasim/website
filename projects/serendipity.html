Serendipity and Decision-Making

The increased engagement in online space has resulted in an information overload that makes it challenging to tease apart critical information from the mundane With the ever-increasing popularity of social media, product reviews, and online engagement platforms, short free-form unstructured text such as tweets, Facebook and Reddit posts, Amazon and Yelp reviews, YouTube and Nextdoor comments are found in abundance. These platforms aim to enable people to share their thoughts and opinions and allow analysts, policymakers, and people in general, to harvest useful information and acquire knowledge to inform decision-making. These decisions can range from buying a car or a house to making critical policy changes based on public input. At a personal level, data-driven decisions can help make effective financial choices or take a political or moral stance in an ongoing argument on social media. From a broader perspective, policy decisions are often made based on public input that can significantly impact peopleâ€™s lives. Although online text comments have significant impacts on the readers' knowledge acquisition, understanding and decision-making process, making sense of the large-scale online free-form text information remains challenging due to the heterogeneity, redundancy, and lack of structure in the text comments along with the ambiguity of human language. As a result, decisions are often made based on an incomplete exploration and understanding of the underlying data. We aim too explore the utility of serendipitious information and how they can impact decision-making at a personal level.
