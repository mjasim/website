Community Input Analysis

Civic leaders often make policy decisions based on large-scale textual data gathered from online community engagement platforms. However, it can be difficult to identify useful information that reflects community perspectives to inform such decisions. As a matter of fact, effective exploration and analysis of unstructured domain-specific textual data is an open challenge that requires closer attention. In this study, we conducted interviews with 14 civic leaders who have an extensive background in gathering, analyzing, and interpreting large-scale community input to gain a deeper understanding of their community input analysis practices, challenges, and requirements. We identified their need for specialized tools to organize the unstructured community input for better analysis and surface community's reflections towards civic proposals that goes beyond positive, negative, or neutral sentiments. Based on these interviews, we aim to design and develop technological interventions that can enable civic leaders to better explore, analyze, and understand community-generated input.
